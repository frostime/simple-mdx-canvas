# Atlas Release Brief

<Callout type="summary" title="Release decision">
Proceed with the limited launch on Tuesday. The remaining work is operational
coordination, not a product-quality blocker.
</Callout>

## The problem

Atlas gives support teams one place to review customer escalation context before
an incident handoff. The release artifact needs to make the decision, evidence,
and next actions easy to scan without requiring a live application.

<Column title="Audience">

</Column>
<Column title="Release scope">

</Column>
</Columns>

## Rollout choices

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Limited launch">

Start with the launch cohort, review the first ten escalation packets, and
expand after the Tuesday and Friday operating reviews.

</Tab>
<Tab title="Full launch">

Release to every support team immediately. This saves coordination time but
increases the blast radius of the CRM synchronization risk.

</Tab>
<Tab title="Hold">

Wait for the CRM sync hardening work. This removes the manual fallback but
delays the validated support workflow.

</Tab>
</Tabs>

## Launch note

<PromptBox title="Message for the launch channel">

```text
Atlas limited launch begins Tuesday at 09:00. Use the escalation packet for all
cohort handoffs. Report CRM synchronization delays in #atlas-launch and include
the packet URL, customer account, and incident priority.
```

</PromptBox>

## First-week checklist

1. Confirm cohort access before Tuesday morning.
2. Review the first ten generated escalation packets with support leads.
3. Track CRM synchronization delays and manual fallback usage.
4. Decide whether to expand the cohort at Friday's operating review.
